Seven years at MoneyView. Five role changes. IC → Lead → Engineering Manager → Senior EM → Director of Engineering. Here's what I've learned about each transition.

## IC → Lead: The Multiplier Shift

As an IC, your output is your code. As a lead, your output is your team's code. The hardest part isn't delegation — it's accepting that "good enough, shipped by the team" beats "perfect, shipped by you alone." You stop being the best coder in the room and start being the person who makes the room better at coding.

## Lead → Manager: The Context Switch

You start spending 60% of your time in meetings, and it feels wrong. Until you realize those meetings ARE the work. Aligning stakeholders, unblocking your team, shaping roadmaps — this is how engineering gets done at scale. The code you write drops to near-zero, and that's okay.

## Manager → Director: The Strategy Layer

The biggest shift: you stop solving problems and start choosing which problems to solve. Your decisions have months-long feedback loops. You're hiring for roles that don't exist yet. You're building the organization, not the product.

What stays the same through all of it: curiosity, systems thinking, and the deep satisfaction of watching something you built serve millions of people.
